**That last one is exactly where the real gap is, and it affects your very next step.** `ensureInitialized()` only re-runs `initialize()` while `this.initialized` is still `false`. Startup already succeeds right now ‚Äî with zero protocols seeded, but it still *succeeds*, so `initialized` flips to `true` immediately. That means once you seed `aave_v3`/`uniswap_v3` protocol rows, `/transactions/classify` will keep returning `adapters: 0` and skip everything, because nothing tells the registry to re-scan the `protocols` table ‚Äî the lazy re-init only covers "startup failed outright" (DB unreachable), not "startup succeeded with a stale/empty adapter map." You'll need either a server restart after seeding, or (better) a small `POST /admin/registry/refresh` route that calls `registry.initialize()` on demand. Worth adding before you seed those protocols, or the first classify call after seeding will look like it silently did nothing.
