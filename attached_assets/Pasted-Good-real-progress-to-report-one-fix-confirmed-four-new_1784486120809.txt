**The real bug — not a citation, a logic error:** `/export/audit-package` and `/export/cpa-handoff` both filter positions by `tax_year` using `p.createdAt` — the database row's creation timestamp, not the date of the underlying transaction. If a 2024 transaction gets classified (or backfilled, or reclassified) in 2026, its `createdAt` is 2026, and it silently won't appear in a 2024 audit package — the one document whose entire purpose is completeness for that tax year. This is the kind of gap between "reported as done" and "actually correct" that matters most: it'll work fine in every demo (positions created same-day as their transactions) and fail exactly when someone needs it for a real audit response covering historical activity.

**Inconsistent "open gap" definitions in the same file:** `/export/pattern-report` hardcodes `OPEN_GAP_EVENT_TYPES` with 6 entries including `bridge_transfer`. `/export/comment-letter` hardcodes a separate `OPEN_GAP_EVENTS` object with only 5, and `bridge_transfer` is missing. So a bridge-transfer position gets flagged as open-gap in the pattern report but silently excluded from the comment-letter export — the one export whose whole job is capturing every open-gap position. Two sources of truth for the same concept, already out of sync.

Seven years is a real, commonly-used practical retention buffer, but it isn't what §6501 says, so citing it that specifically is the same kind of misattribution as the T.D. mix-up, just not yet caught.
