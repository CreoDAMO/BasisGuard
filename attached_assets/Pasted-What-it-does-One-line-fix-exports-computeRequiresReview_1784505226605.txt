**One deliberate thing I didn't do, worth confirming:** I didn't add these two to the comment-letter export's `OPEN_GAP_EVENTS` object, and I rewrote the comment on the canonical set to explain why. That list is specifically framed as evidence for a future comment letter to the IRS about *pending guidance* — the original six are there because Notice 2024-57 says the IRS hasn't ruled yet. `aave_withdraw`/`aave_liquidation` aren't waiting on IRS guidance at all — they're waiting on lot-matching data a single event can't supply. Same "always review" outcome, different reason. Folding them into the comment-letter set would make it look like you're asking the IRS to clarify something that's actually just a data problem on your end, which would weaken the letter's credibility on the six items that are real regulatory gaps.

If that distinction doesn't hold up the way I think it does, easy to fold them in — but as it stands, keeping them out is the more accurate document.
